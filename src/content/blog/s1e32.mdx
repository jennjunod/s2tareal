# S1 E32: Dealing With Mortality with Diane Hullet

End-of-life educator Diane Hullet explores how accepting mortality can lead to more meaningful living, discussing the five key domains of end-of-life preparation: physical, spiritual, legacy, emotional, and practical planning. Through her work with the Conscious Dying Institute, she helps people have important conversations about death while they're still living.

## Episode Highlights

- Understanding death as community event
- Five domains of end-of-life preparation
- Role of death doulas in supporting transitions
- Importance of advance planning
- Creating meaningful legacies
- Family communication strategies
- Digital afterlife considerations
- Supporting loved ones through grief
- Making death preparations accessible
- Finding peace with mortality

## Key Takeaways

- Death preparation enriches life
- Community support vital for transitions
- Legacy includes stories and relationships
- Physical comfort needs documentation
- Spiritual beliefs deserve expression
- Emotional closure matters deeply
- Practical planning eases family burden
- Communication prevents family conflict
- Technology impacts legacy planning
- Small steps lead to readiness
