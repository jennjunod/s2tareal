# S1 E4: Suicide Prevention with Louisa Rocque

In this crucial episode, Louisa Rocque from the American Foundation for Suicide Prevention discusses the importance of open conversations about mental health and suicide prevention. She shares professional insights and personal experiences about supporting those in crisis and breaking down stigma around mental health discussions.

## Episode Highlights

- The importance of being listeners rather than fixers when supporting others
- Understanding warning signs and how to respond appropriately
- The power of authentic conversations in reducing mental health stigma
- Resources available through AFSP and other support organizations
- The upcoming implementation of 988 as the new suicide prevention lifeline
- Creating safe and secure spaces for difficult conversations

## Key Takeaways

- Mental health conversations require empathy and active listening
- Support looks different for everyone - there's no one-size-fits-all approach
- Professional help, like therapy, is a journey of finding the right fit
- Self-care and boundaries are crucial for both those struggling and their supporters
- The importance of checking in on even your "strong" friends
